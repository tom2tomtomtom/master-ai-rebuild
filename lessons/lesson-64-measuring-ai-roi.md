**Problem 1: Can't Measure Time Savings (Too Variable)**

*Symptom*:
- Different people take different times
- Task complexity varies
- Hard to isolate AI impact

*Solution - Sample + Aggregate:*
```
Don't try to measure exactly
Do measure sample + aggregate

Method:
- Track 50 instances of task (before)
- Track 50 instances with AI (after)
- Calculate average time per instance
- Multiply: Instances per month Ã— time saved

Example:
Sales email:
- Sample 50 emails before AI: Average 15 minutes
- Sample 50 emails with AI: Average 5 minutes
- Instances per rep per month: 200 emails
- Time saved per rep: 200 Ã— 10 min = 2,000 minutes = 33 hours/month
- Value: 33 hours Ã— $60 = $2,000/month per rep
```
```

**Problem 2: Quality Improvements Hard to Quantify**

*Symptom*:
- "It's better, but I can't measure it"
- Deal close rate improved but many variables
- Customer satisfaction up but unclear why

*Solution - Establish Causation:*
```
Approach:
1. Measure before & after (same sample size, similar period)
2. Control for variables (season, market, etc.)
3. Calculate: Difference attributable to AI

Example - Deal close rate:
Before AI: 20% close rate on 50 deals = 10 deals
With AI: 23% close rate on 50 deals = 11.5 deals (estimated)
Improvement: 1.5 deals
Value: 1.5 Ã— $500K average = $750K

Then: Acknowledge other variables
"Close rate improved 3%. Some from better proposals (AI), some from market."
Conservative estimate: 50% from AI = $375K value
```
