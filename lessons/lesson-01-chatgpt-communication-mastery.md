### Common Issues & Solutions

**Problem 1: "GPT-5's emails sound too formal/robotic"**  
**Solution:** You haven't provided enough voice examples. Give GPT-5 5-10 emails you've actually written and specify your style ("conversational," "direct," "uses light humor"). Add constraints: "Use contractions," "Keep sentences under 20 words," "Sound like a human, not a corporate template."  
**Why it happens:** GPT-5 defaults to generic "professional" tone without specific guidance.

**Problem 2: "The AI misunderstood the context and drafted wrong response"**  
**Solution:** Provide more context in your prompt. Include: (1) relationship with recipient, (2) previous conversation history, (3) specific goal, (4) what NOT to say/do. Use: "Context you need to know: [DETAILS]" section.  
**Why it happens:** AI can't read your mind or past emailsâ€”be explicit about nuance.

**Problem 3: "Drafts are too long / too short"**  
**Solution:** Add specific length constraints: "Exactly 100 words," "3 paragraphs max," "Under 150 words." If output doesn't match, say "Rewrite this at exactly [LENGTH]" as a follow-up.  
**Why it happens:** GPT-5's default email length is ~200 words; you need to override for brevity.

**Problem 4: "I spend too much time editing AI draftsâ€”faster to write myself"**  
**Solution:** You're using AI as a first draft tool, but you should use it as a 90% complete tool. Improve your prompts with: better voice examples, specific phrase requirements ("Start with..." / "Never say..."), format specifications. Aim for drafts that need < 2 minutes of editing.  
**Why it happens:** Insufficient prompt specificity. Better prompt = less editing.

**Problem 5: "Gmail integration isn't catching all my emails"**  
**Solution:** Refine your category triggers. Be more specific: Instead of "meeting requests," use "emails containing: 'meeting,' 'call,' 'schedule,' 'coffee,' 'catch up,' or 'time to chat.'" Test with real examples and adjust.  
**Why it happens:** Email language is diverse; start specific, expand as you see patterns.
