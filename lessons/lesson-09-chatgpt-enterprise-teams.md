## üîß Troubleshooting & Pro Tips

### Common Issues & Solutions

**Problem 1: "Low adoption‚Äîpeople aren't using it"**
**Solution:** Adoption is a marketing problem, not a product problem. Run internal campaign: Success stories in all-hands, lunch & learns with demos, champions in each department evangelizing. Make it visible, social, and celebrated. Gamify: "Most creative use case of the month" contest.
**Why it happens:** People don't change behavior without clear benefit and social proof.

**Problem 2: "Users say 'I don't know what to use it for'"**
**Solution:** Start with ONE specific use case per role. Don't say "use AI for anything"‚Äîsay "Sales: use this for outreach emails. Support: use this for ticket responses." Specific beats generic. Build use case library with examples.
**Why it happens:** Too much flexibility is paralyzing without guidance.

**Problem 3: "Security team blocks everything"**
**Solution:** Involve security early in planning, not at deployment. Show them ChatGPT Enterprise security docs (SOC 2, BAA, zero retention). Configure restrictive policies first, loosen gradually. Start with low-risk departments (marketing, internal ops) to prove safety.
**Why it happens:** Security defaults to "no" without understanding enterprise controls.

**Problem 4: "Can't measure ROI‚Äîusage is too varied"**
**Solution:** Use proxies: Time saved per use case √ó Usage volume = Total value. Survey users monthly: "How many hours did ChatGPT save you this week?" Track specific workflows: Emails sent via AI, docs summarized, etc. Focus on directional ROI, not precise calculation.
**Why it happens:** AI productivity gains are hard to measure directly, but approximation is good enough.

**Problem 5: "Executives don't use it, so teams don't take it seriously"**
**Solution:** Train executives separately (30-min 1-on-1 sessions). Show THEM how it helps with their specific work (board decks, strategic memos, exec communication). When CEO uses ChatGPT publicly, adoption skyrockets. Top-down matters.
**Why it happens:** Culture change requires visible leadership buy-in.

### Pro Tips from Experts

**üí° Power Technique 1: "Champions Network"**
Recruit 1-2 power users per department as "AI Champions." They get advanced training, test new features first, and become peer support for their teams. Way more effective than top-down IT training.

**When to use:** Companies 50+ people where IT can't scale 1-on-1 support
**Expected impact:** 2-3x faster adoption, higher quality use cases, sustainable culture change

**üí° Power Technique 2: "Use Case Library as Onboarding"**
Don't train people on "how to use ChatGPT"‚Äîgive them 50 copy-paste templates for their role. They learn by doing, not by watching demos. Organize by role (Sales, Marketing, Support) and function (Email, Research, Analysis).

**When to use:** All enterprise deployments
**Expected impact:** 80%+ adoption in first month vs. 30-40% with generic training

**üí° Power Technique 3: "AI Office Hours" (Weekly or Bi-Weekly)**
Host live sessions where anyone can join, ask questions, share use cases, troubleshoot. Builds community, surfaces issues early, creates social proof (others see it working). Record and share async.

**When to use:** During rollout phase (first 3-6 months), then monthly ongoing
**Expected impact:** Higher engagement, faster problem resolution, community building

**üí° Power Technique 4: "Executive Briefing: 15-Min AI Demos"**
For each executive, schedule 15 min. Show them ONE high-value use case for their job (CFO: financial analysis, CMO: campaign briefs, CTO: code review). Get them hooked personally. They become advocates.

**When to use:** Before company-wide rollout, to build top-down support
**Expected impact:** Executive sponsorship, budget approval, cultural permission

**üí° Power Technique 5: "AI Newsletter: Weekly Wins"**
Send company-wide email every week: "This week's AI wins" with 3-5 success stories from employees. Social proof + inspiration + FOMO. Shows what's possible, celebrates users, drives adoption.

**When to use:** Ongoing (first 6 months critical)
**Expected impact:** Sustained engagement, continuous learning, culture shift
