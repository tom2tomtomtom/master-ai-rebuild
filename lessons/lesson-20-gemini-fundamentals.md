## ðŸ”§ Troubleshooting & Pro Tips

### 5 Common Gemini Problems & Solutions

**Problem 1: "Gemini's research seems inaccurate or outdated"**

**Root Cause**: Gemini searches the internet but older sources sometimes outrank current ones. Or you're not prompting specifically for recency.

**Solution**:
1. Add recency requirement to prompt: "Focus on information from the last 30 days. Prioritize official announcements over secondary reporting."
2. Verify sources: Check the dates Gemini provides. If most sources are 3+ months old, the topic may not have recent changes.
3. Cross-reference: For critical decisions, verify in 2-3 sources before relying on Gemini's synthesis.
4. Be specific: "What changed in the last 90 days?" gets better recency than "What's happening with [company]?"

**Example fix**:
```
Instead of: "Research competitor pricing"
Use: "What are [Competitor]'s current pricing changes announced in the last 90 days?
Focus on official announcements and press releases."
```

---

**Problem 2: "Gemini gives too much information; I need a shorter answer"**

**Root Cause**: Gemini's real-time search pulls diverse information. Without length constraints, it synthesizes broadly.

**Solution**:
1. Specify format: "Output: Single-page executive summary with 5 bullet points"
2. Define priority: "Focus on: [top 3 things you care about], skip other details"
3. Set constraints: "Limit to critical findings only. Exclude background/history unless essential."
4. Use templates: Templates force structured output (don't use free-form prompts)

**Example fix**:
```
Instead of: "Research our competitor"
Use: "Research [Competitor]. Output format: 1-page summary with sections for:
- Current product positioning (3 bullets)
- Recent announcements (last 90 days, 3 bullets)
- Key gaps vs us (3 bullets)
- Sales talking points (3 bullets)
Skip background, history, or detailed feature comparisons."
```

---

**Problem 3: "I'm not sure if Gemini's sources are credible"**

**Root Cause**: Gemini includes secondary sources and analysis pieces that may not be authoritative.

**Solution**:
1. Source evaluation: Prefer official announcements, press releases, company blogs over news articles
2. Corroboration: Check if the key finding appears in 2+ sources
3. Recency check: Recent official sources trump older secondary sources
4. Explicit request: "Use only official sources: press releases, investor announcements, company blogs. Avoid speculation or rumor."

**Example fix**:
```
Instead of: "Is [Competitor] launching a new product?"
Use: "Has [Competitor] announced plans to launch a new product in [category]?
Source requirement: Use only official announcements (press releases, investor calls, company blog).
If no official announcement found, state 'no official announcement found.'"
```

---

**Problem 4: "Gemini can't access my Google Sheets data"**

**Root Cause**: You need to explicitly paste data or have Gemini integrated with your Workspace (which requires specific setup).

**Solution** (workaround):
1. Copy your Sheets data (select, copy)
2. Paste directly into Gemini prompt with context
3. Tell Gemini: "Below is my data from Google Sheets [table]. Analyze this alongside [external data]."
4. For deeper integration: Use Gemini for Workspace (if you have it) or use copy-paste method

**Best practice**:
```
MY DATA (from Google Sheets):
[Paste your table with headers and rows]

RESEARCH REQUEST:
Please combine this data with real-time research on [topic] and provide analysis.
Specifically: How does our performance compare to [competitor/benchmark]?
```

---

**Problem 5: "I'm not getting actionable recommendations from Gemini"**

**Root Cause**: You're asking Gemini to research but not asking for recommendations. Gemini needs explicit guidance to synthesize into action.

**Solution**:
1. Always include "RECOMMENDATIONS:" section asking what to do with findings
2. Give context: "This informs [specific decision we're making]"
3. Specify action framework: "What should we do in next 30 days based on this?"
4. Ask for prioritization: "What's most important? What's urgent?"

**Example fix**:
```
Instead of: "What's happening in the market?"
Use: "Research market changes in [space]. Based on what you find:

1. STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS: What does this mean for our business?
2. COMPETITIVE THREAT: Are we at risk from these changes?
3. GROWTH OPPORTUNITY: Is there a market opening for us?
4. 30-DAY ACTION PLAN: What should we do about this?
5. DECISION FRAMEWORK: What should trigger urgent action?"
```
