### Level 1: Foundation (5 minutes) - Multi-Step Problem Solving

**Scenario**: Your analytics team has a dataset with anomalies they can't explain. Sales dropped 8% last monthâ€”unexpected. Was it seasonal? Competition? Marketing change? Product issue? Market downturn? You need to work through this systematically to find the real cause.

**Mission**: Use reasoning model to systematically diagnose the sales drop problem.

**Step-by-Step Foundation Exercise:**

**Step 1: Frame the Problem**
Define what you're trying to solve:
```
PROBLEM TO SOLVE:
Monthly sales dropped 8% last month (Oct vs Sept)

DATA WE HAVE:
- Month-over-month sales figures (last 12 months)
- Customer churn rate (Oct: 3.2%, Sept: 2.8%)
- New customer acquisition (Oct: 45 new, Sept: 52 new)
- Average order value (Oct: $2,100, Sept: $2,150)
- Website traffic (Oct: 25K visitors, Sept: 27K visitors)
- Marketing spend (Oct: $15K, Sept: $15K)
- Product changes (none in October)
- Market events (competitors launched new product Oct 1)
- Seasonal patterns (Oct typically slow? No, usually normal)

WHAT WE WANT TO UNDERSTAND:
1. What caused the sales drop?
2. Is this temporary or ongoing?
3. What should we do about it?
```

**Step 2: Ask Reasoning Model to Analyze**
```
Using the data above, work through this step-by-step:

1. ANALYZE THE METRICS
   - What's driving the 8% sales drop?
   - Which metrics changed? By how much?
   - Which metric changes explain the sales drop?

2. ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS
   - What's the most likely root cause?
   - Could multiple factors be contributing?
   - What's the evidence for each potential cause?

3. SEASONAL VS TREND
   - Is Oct normally a slow month? (Check historical data)
   - Is this a temporary anomaly or start of trend?
   - What patterns do you see?

4. CONTRIBUTING FACTORS
   - Churn increase: +0.4% = [X% sales impact]
   - New customers down: -7 customers = [X% sales impact]
   - AOV down: -$50 = [X% sales impact]
   - Traffic down: -2K visitors = [X% sales impact]
   - Total impact: Does this explain 8% drop?

5. EXTERNAL FACTORS
   - Did competitor product launch cause this?
   - Are there market conditions we're missing?
   - Did any communication/PR happen that week?

6. RECOMMENDATION
   - Most likely cause?
   - Supporting evidence?
   - What should we investigate further?
   - What actions should we take?

Show your reasoning at each step
```

**Step 3: Interpret the Analysis**
Reasoning model provides:
- Systematic walkthrough of metrics
- Evidence for each potential cause
- Root cause identification with confidence level
- Recommended next steps

Example analysis:
```
ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS:

Metric changes in October:
1. Churn: 2.8% â†’ 3.2% (+0.4%) = -$8,400/month impact
2. New customers: 52 â†’ 45 (-7 customers) = -$14,700/month impact
3. AOV: $2,150 â†’ $2,100 (-$50) = -$1,250/month impact
4. Traffic: 27K â†’ 25K (-2K visitors) = -$7,400/month impact

Total identified impact: -$32K (vs -$42K actual)
Gap: Other factors explain remaining 23% of decline

ANALYSIS:
The sales drop is driven by three factors:
1. Increased churn (customers leaving) = 20% of decline
2. Reduced new customer acquisition = 35% of decline
3. Reduced traffic (fewer prospects) = 18% of decline
4. Unidentified factors = 27% of decline

MOST LIKELY ROOT CAUSE:
Competitor product launch on Oct 1 caused:
- Existing customers to evaluate competitor â†’ higher churn
- Reduced marketing effectiveness â†’ fewer new customers
- Possible brand shift in market perception â†’ lower traffic

CONFIDENCE: 70% (can confirm if we check competitor reviews, customer feedback)

RECOMMENDATION:
1. Survey lost customers: Why did they leave?
2. Check competitor reviews: Are customers switching?
3. Analyze customer feedback: Is competitor being mentioned?
4. Review marketing metrics: Conversion rate trends?
5. If competitor is cause: Develop competitive response plan
```

**Step 4: Develop Action Plan**
Based on analysis, take action:
```
IF COMPETITOR IS ROOT CAUSE:
1. Analyze competitor advantages (features, pricing, positioning)
2. Develop competitive response (product, marketing, pricing)
3. Proactive customer retention (special offers, feature upgrades)
4. Messaging shift (emphasize your differentiators vs. competitor)

IF MARKET DOWNTURN IS CAUSE:
1. Focus on retention (protect existing customers)
2. Reduce customer acquisition cost (focus on ROI)
3. Upsell to existing customers (increase AOV)

MEASUREMENT:
Track these metrics daily/weekly:
- Customer churn rate (should stabilize)
- New customer acquisition (should increase)
- AOV (should stabilize)
- Website traffic (should increase if marketing working)
- Competitor review sentiment (should reveal if they're the issue)
```

### Level 2: Intermediate (7 minutes) - Strategic Decision Analysis

**Scenario**: Your company faces a strategic decision: We have $2M in capital. Do we: A) Hire sales/marketing team (expand market presence), B) Build new product features (product leadership), or C) Acquire competitor (consolidation). Each option has tradeoffs. **Mission**: Use reasoning model to analyze strategic options and recommend best path forward.

**Step-by-Step Intermediate Exercise:**

**Step 1: Define Decision Parameters**
Create comprehensive decision framework:
```

Timeline: 3-month ramp
Expected outcome: Revenue growth 30-50% in year 1
Risks: Sales team productivity varies, competitive responses
Upside: Market penetration, customer base growth
Downside: Fixed costs, revenue depends on sales execution

OPTION B: Product Feature Development
Investment: $2M (hire 4-5 engineers + 1-2 product managers)
Timeline: 6-12 months for features, then market adoption
Expected outcome: Product leadership, premium positioning
Risks: Development delays, market may not want features
Upside: Sustainable competitive advantage, premium pricing
Downside: Long payoff timeline, development risk

OPTION C: Competitor Acquisition
Investment: $2M acquisition + $500K integration
Timeline: 3-6 months integration
Expected outcome: Market share consolidation, cost synergies
Risks: Integration challenges, culture clash, key team departures
Upside: Eliminate competitor, immediate scale
Downside: Integration risk, management attention diverted

KEY DECISION CRITERIA:
1. Financial return: Which option highest ROI?
2. Speed: Which gets results fastest?
3. Risk: Which is lowest risk?
4. Strategic fit: Which aligns with company vision?
5. Execution: Which can we actually execute well?
6. Competitive position: Which most improves competitive standing?

COMPANY CONTEXT:
- Current revenue: $5M ARR
- Growth target: $10M by end of year 2
- Competitive position: Strong product, limited sales reach
- Team strengths: Product development, weak sales execution
- Market conditions: Growing category, 5+ competitors
- Financial runway: 18 months (even without revenue growth)
```

**Step 2: Ask Reasoning Model for Analysis**
```
STRATEGIC OPTION ANALYSIS:

For each option (A, B, C), analyze:

1. FINANCIAL ANALYSIS
   - Investment required?
   - Expected revenue impact by month?
   - Break-even timeline?
   - 2-year ROI?
   - Confidence in projections?

2. EXECUTION CAPABILITY
   - Can our team execute this?
   - What are execution risks?
   - What could go wrong?
   - Probability of success?

3. COMPETITIVE IMPACT
   - How does this improve competitive position?
   - How will competitors respond?
   - Is this defensible?
   - Long-term vs. short-term advantage?

4. STRATEGIC ALIGNMENT
   - Does this align with company vision?
   - Does this leverage our strengths?
   - Does this address our weaknesses?
   - Is this sustainable?

5. OPPORTUNITY COST
   - What do we give up by choosing this?
   - What opportunities are we missing?
   - Could we do this later?
   - If we don't do this now, what happens?

6. RECOMMENDATION FRAMEWORK
   - Rank options by: ROI, Speed, Risk, Strategic fit
   - Which option best serves company goals?
   - What are the caveats/assumptions?
   - What could change the recommendation?

SHOW YOUR THINKING: Work through this systematically
```

**Step 3: Evaluate Recommendation**
Model provides:
- Financial analysis of each option with projections
- Execution risk assessment
- Competitive analysis
- Strategic alignment assessment
- Ranked recommendation with caveats

Example analysis structure:
```
FINANCIAL COMPARISON:

Option A - Sales Expansion:
- Investment: $2M
- Year 1 revenue increase: $1.5M-2M (30-40% growth)
- Year 2 revenue increase: $2.5M-3M (additional)
- Breakeven: Month 6-8
- 2-year ROI: 150-200%

Option B - Product Development:
- Investment: $2M
- Year 1 revenue impact: $200-500K (features enable premium pricing)
- Year 2 revenue impact: $1.5M-2.5M (market adoption)
- Breakeven: Month 12-18
- 2-year ROI: 50-100%

Option C - Acquisition:
- Investment: $2.5M total
- Year 1 revenue: Consolidate competitor's customers (+$1M)
- Year 1 cost savings: $300-500K (eliminate duplicate roles)
- Breakeven: Month 4-6 (including synergies)
- 2-year ROI: 80-120%

RANKING BY METRICS:
Fastest ROI: Option A (6-8 months)
Highest 2-year ROI: Option A (150-200%)
Lowest Risk: Option B (development risk but controllable)
Strategic Advantage: Option B (builds moat)
Execution Confidence: Option A (sales team, proven model)

RECOMMENDATION:
Best option depends on company priorities:
- If prioritizing growth speed: Option A
- If prioritizing sustainable advantage: Option B
- If prioritizing market consolidation: Option C

CAVEAT: Recommendation assumes:
- Sales team hires are high-quality
- Product features are what market wants
- Competitor acquisition target is willing seller
- Company culture can integrate acquisition

RISK MITIGATION:
- Option A: Hire proven sales manager first
- Option B: Validate feature demand before dev
- Option C: Due diligence on target, retention plans
```

**Step 4: Make Decision with Confidence**
Analysis provides:
- Clear understanding of each option
- Financial impact quantified
- Risks identified and assessed
- Recommendation with reasoning

### 5 Common Reasoning Model Problems & Solutions

**Problem 1: "Model takes too long to analyze; I need quick answer"**

**Root Cause**: Reasoning models work through problems thoroughly, which takes time.

**Solution**:
1. For quick answers: Use regular (not thinking) model
2. For complex problems: Accept 30-60 sec analysis time (vs. hours manually)
3. Specify time constraint: "Think through this quickly, provide analysis in 30 seconds"
4. Stream output: Show thinking as it progresses, don't wait for completion

---

**Problem 2: "Model's reasoning is hard to follow; I don't understand its logic"**

**Root Cause**: Model's thinking is complex and not always clearly explained.

**Solution**:
1. Ask for clearer explanation: "Explain your reasoning in simpler terms"
2. Ask to break down: "Walk me through this step-by-step"
3. Ask for examples: "Give an example to illustrate your reasoning"
4. Highlight confusion: "This step is unclear to me. Explain..."

---

**Problem 3: "Model recommends something I disagree with; how do I know it's right?"**

**Root Cause**: Model recommendation may be based on different assumptions or priorities.

**Solution**:
1. Ask for reasoning: "Why do you recommend this over the alternative?"
2. Challenge assumptions: "What if [your assumption] instead?"
3. Get confidence level: "How confident are you in this recommendation?"
4. Ask for alternatives: "What if I prioritize [different goal]?"
5. Validate independently: Model is advisor, not decision-maker

---

**Problem 4: "Model generates very long analysis; I just need the answer"**

**Root Cause**: Reasoning models show all thinking, which can be very long.

**Solution**:
1. Ask for summary first: "Summarize your recommendation in 1 sentence, then explain"
2. Specify format: "Give me: Recommendation | Reasoning | Next Steps (3 bullets each)"
3. Ask for executive summary: "1-page analysis focusing on decision, not all details"
4. Read selectively: Skim reasoning, focus on conclusion

---

**Problem 5: "I'm not sure which problems need reasoning models; when to use them?"**

**Root Cause**: Not clear on when deep reasoning helps vs. simple analysis.

**Solution**:
Use reasoning models for:
- âœ“ Complex decisions with multiple tradeoffs
- âœ“ Root cause analysis of complex problems
- âœ“ Strategic planning and long-term decisions
- âœ“ Research synthesis across many sources
- âœ“ Problems with multiple interconnected factors
- âœ“ When you need to understand the "why" not just the "what"

Don't use reasoning models for:
- âœ— Simple factual questions (just ask regular model)
- âœ— When you need instant answers (takes 30-60 seconds)
- âœ— When the problem is straightforward (overkill)
- âœ— For routine/familiar tasks (regular model is fine)
