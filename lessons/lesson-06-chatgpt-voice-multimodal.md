### Common Issues & Solutions:

**Problem 1: "Voice mode isn't understanding me accurately"**
**Solution:**
- Speak at moderate pace (not too fast)
- Use clear enunciation, especially for technical terms
- Spell out unusual names: "That's J-O-H-N-S-O-N"
- Reduce background noise (car wind, office chatter)
- Use better microphone (earbuds often better than phone mic)

**Why it happens:** Voice recognition struggles with fast speech, background noise, and uncommon terms

---

**Problem 2: "Voice responses are too long/verbose"**
**Solution:**
- Add to your prompt: "Keep responses concise, under 30 seconds"
- Interrupt with: "Give me the short version"
- Use: "Bullet points only" for structured lists
- For very brief: "One sentence answer only"

**Why it happens:** Voice mode defaults to conversational style (vs. text which can be skimmed). You control verbosity.

---

**Problem 3: "Can't capture/save the voice conversation"**
**Solution:**
- End every voice session with: "Summarize our conversation in text and send it to me"
- ChatGPT will post text summary to your chat history
- Screenshot or copy-paste for external use
- For automatic capture: use Custom GPTs with "always provide text summary at end"

**Why it happens:** Voice is ephemeral by default; you must explicitly request text capture

---

**Problem 4: "Voice mode disconnects or stops listening"**
**Solution:**
- Check app is updated to latest version
- Ensure stable internet connection (voice requires more bandwidth than text)
- On mobile: prevent app from sleeping (adjust battery settings)
- If persistent: toggle voice mode off/on to reset
- Desktop: check browser microphone permissions

**Why it happens:** Network issues, app bugs, or permission conflicts

---

**Problem 5: "Difficulty switching between voice and typing mid-conversation"**
**Solution:**
- You CAN mix modes seamlessly: speak one message, type the next
- To stop voice listening: tap the stop button
- To resume voice: tap microphone icon
- For complex edits: switch to typing, then back to voice
- No need to maintain one mode for entire conversation

**Why it happens:** Users assume they must stick with one modeâ€”actually fully interchangeable
