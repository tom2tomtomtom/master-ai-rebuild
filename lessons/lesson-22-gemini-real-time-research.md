## ðŸ”§ Troubleshooting & Pro Tips

### 5 Common Real-Time Research Problems & Solutions

**Problem 1: "Gemini's 'real-time' research shows old news, not actual breaking news"**

**Root Cause**: "Real-time" means internet-connected, but Gemini prioritizes authoritative sources which sometimes lag. Also, you need to be specific about timeframe.

**Solution**:
1. Specify date requirements: "From the last 24 hours only"
2. Ask for announcement sources: "Focus on official announcements and press releases"
3. Verify dates: Gemini provides sources with datesâ€”check them
4. Follow up: "What happened more recently?" to push for latest info
5. Use multiple searches: Research multiple angles for breaking news

**Example fix**:
```
Instead of: "What's happening with [company]?"
Use: "What announcement or news about [company] was published in the last 24 hours?
     Focus on official sources: press releases, official statements, investor updates.
     Include date for each item found."
```

---

**Problem 2: "Signal monitoring is creating too many false positives (not all signals = buying opportunity)"**

**Root Cause**: You're measuring signals too broadly or not filtering for your specific buying criteria.

**Solution**:
1. Define signal + timeframe combination: Don't just "funding" but "Series B funding in fintech in US"
2. Add qualification criteria: "Only companies with 50-500 employees with growth signals"
3. Time thresholds: "Search for signals in last 14 days only" (stale signals aren't urgent)
4. Score signals by likelihood: "What's the probability this company has buying need?"
5. Post-signal timing: "When typically do companies buy after [signal]?"

**Example fix**:
```
Instead of: [Any funding signal] "Send outreach immediately"
Use: [Series A-C funding in target industry in target size range, announced in last 14 days]
     Score: High probability buying need (95%) if they hired sales/product leadership
     Timing: 2-4 weeks post-funding (when budget allocated and planning starts)
     Qualification: Only if company is growing (vs. raising to extend runway)
```

---

**Problem 3: "The intelligence briefing is too long/too much information"**

**Root Cause**: You didn't specify conciseness constraints or Gemini is providing comprehensive coverage when you need executive summary.

**Solution**:
1. Specify length: "Executive brief: max 1 page"
2. Specify format: "5 bullet points maximum"
3. Prioritize: "Focus on: top 3 news items only"
4. Use templates: Structured formats enforce brevity
5. Separate by audience: "Executive brief (1 page)" vs. "Detailed analysis (3 pages)"

**Example fix**:
```
Instead of: "Brief me on market news"
Use: "One-page executive briefing on market news this week.
     Format: 3 bullet points max per section
     Sections: Top 3 news items | Key implications | Recommended actions
     Total: 1 page max, scannable"
```

---

**Problem 4: "The real-time research sometimes includes rumor/speculation mixed with facts"**

**Root Cause**: Gemini includes news articles that include speculation or rumors alongside facts.

**Solution**:
1. Request source specification: "Use only official sources (press releases, company blogs, earnings calls)"
2. Mark speculation: "If any item is rumor/speculation, clearly mark as such vs. confirmed"
3. Require corroboration: "Only include items from 2+ sources"
4. Exclude speculation keywords: "Avoid: 'rumored', 'reported', 'alleged', unless confirmed"
5. Verify critical items: For decisions, verify facts before acting

**Example fix**:
```
Instead of: "Research [competitor]"
Use: "Research [competitor]. Report sources for all claims.
     Official sources preferred: press releases, investor updates, company blogs.
     News articles only if corroborated by 2+ sources.
     Mark any speculation/rumor clearly and separately from confirmed facts."
```

---

**Problem 5: "Intelligence briefing is not actionable; I don't know what to do with the information"**

**Root Cause**: Intelligence without implications and recommendations is just data. You need to synthesize into action.

**Solution**:
1. Always include "What does this mean for us?" section
2. Always include "What should we do?" recommendations
3. Specify urgency: "Does this need response this week? This month?"
4. Assign owner: "Who should handle this?"
5. Set timeline: "When should we decide/act?"

**Example fix**:
```
Instead of: "Report what happened this week"
Use: "For each development this week:

1. WHAT HAPPENED (facts)
2. WHY IT MATTERS (implications for us)
3. WHAT WE SHOULD DO (recommendations)
4. URGENCY (this week/month/can wait)
5. OWNER (who decides/acts?)
6. TIMELINE (when?)

Make it clear what action we should take based on intelligence."
```

### 5 Power Techniques for Advanced Real-Time Research

**Technique 1: Predictive Intelligence (Forecast, Don't Just React)**

Use real-time research to predict trends before they're obvious:

```
PREDICTIVE INTELLIGENCE PROMPT:
Based on current trends and signals, what's likely to happen next?

Current signals: [List 10-15 recent news items]

Output: Predictive analysis with confidence levels and timeframes
```

**Benefit**: Move from reactive ("What happened?") to proactive ("What will happen?").

**Technique 2: Competitive War Room Intelligence**

```
COMPETITIVE WAR ROOM BRIEF:

- Our relevant capabilities: [list]

- Our vulnerabilities: [list]

```

**Benefit**: Structured response to competitive threats instead of panic mode.

**Benefit**: Not just what changed, but HOW FAST it's changing (velocity = strategic signal).

```
GREEN FLAG TRIGGERS (evaluate if opportunity):
- Market trend acceleration (signals growth opportunity)

Monitoring: Check thresholds daily/weekly
Action: Route alerts to decision-maker automatically
```

**Benefit**: Focus on signals that matter; ignore noise.
